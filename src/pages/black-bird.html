The Black Bird

A black bird appears beside a body. The scene does not remain singular. It draws other materials toward it, each carrying its own form and degree of support. The graph lets the reader move among them, while the Reader gives one selected object time.

CONTEXT

A field that keeps its differences

The Black Bird develops the hypergraph form of speculative research poetry by giving each material a distinct working body. Research notes carry source-bearing prose. Mapping notes condense charged relations into local textual surfaces. Recurring things receive stable addresses, while names and citations preserve their own routes of return.

These distinctions let research and poetry remain in contact without asking one to borrow the authority of the other. The field makes contact spatial. The Reader gives one object time. Route records movement, so a reading can remain partial and still leave a trace.
