Grave-Machine

Grave-Machine is a generative poem drawn from the dramatic world of Grave. The work places bodily remains and administrative processing inside the same recurring procedure. On one side, the poem continues. On the other, a quieter trace records how each line arrived.

CONTEXT

From dramatic material to generative return

Grave was written as the practical component of Mohammad Zare's MA in Dramatic Literature. Its scenes place bodies beside paperwork. Rooms become processing sites, and workers move through an environment where burial begins to resemble administration.

Grave-Machine does not adapt the play as plot. It extracts a field of pressure and gives it a new temporal form. Words return under changing arrangements; pauses interrupt accumulation; the surface keeps moving without offering a final administrative closure.

A transformed lineage

The work also belongs to the remix lineage opened by Nick Montfort's Taroko Gorge. It inherits the proposition of a readable poem-engine, then changes the ground it carries. Bodies return as records. Graves become sites of procedure. Return itself remains unfinished.
