About

Mozare is the artistic name used for the works. Mohammad Zare remains the name used for academic records, citations, rights, and professional identification.

Mohammad Zare is an Iranian poet, researcher, and product/interface practitioner. His work joins dramatic literature to browser-based forms. He is particularly interested in moments when writing remains visibly attached to its sources, its procedures, or the actions of its reader.

He studied Industrial Engineering at Sharif University of Technology and completed an MA in Dramatic Literature at the University of Art, Tehran. His MA research examined ugliness in the plays of Abbas Nalbandian through Deleuze and Guattari's concept of minor literature. Its practical component was the original play Grave, developed beside the theoretical research as an autonomous dramatic work.

His broader practice began in playwriting and speculative research poetry, then extended through critical writing, translation, and curatorial work. Product and interface design contribute another discipline: data structures must remain coherent, complex behavior must be testable, and a surface must make its structure usable without erasing it.

The four works in this portfolio are where those trajectories currently meet. They are presented under the name Mozare. The formal name, Mohammad Zare, remains attached to the CV and to academic, citation, rights, and professional records.
